Principle of Nanopore sequencing

Nanopore sequencing technology measures the current signal by translocating a DNA/RNA molecule through a nanoscale pore anchored on a lipid membrane, as illustrated in Fig. 1. The shape, size, and chemical properties of nucleotides in the pore jointly determine the current signals, which are collected and utilized to infer the nucleotide sequence using computational methods (e.g., seq2seq deep learning models). There are multiple nucleotides (usually 5) in the pore, which we term as kmer (e.g., 5mer). The inferred $k$mers are assembled into a DNA/RNA sequence computationally, which completes the sequencing process.
